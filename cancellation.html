Transparency First

Cancellation
Policy

Life happens. We understand. Here's exactly what to expect if your plans change — written plainly, with no hidden clauses.

🤝

We've kept this policy as simple and fair as possible. All charges are calculated on the total trek cost at time of booking. If you need to cancel or have concerns, please reach out to us directly — we'll always try to find a solution that works for everyone.

When You Cancel

Cancellation Timeline

More than 30 Days Before Departure
5% service charge

Cancellations made more than 30 days ahead give us enough time to rearrange logistics without significant loss. We retain only a small 5% service charge to cover administrative and banking costs already incurred on your booking.

✓  You receive a 95% refund of your total trek cost.
7 to 30 Days Before Departure
20% service charge

Within this window, guides have been confirmed, teahouses reserved, and permits often arranged. A 20% charge reflects the costs that are already committed and non-recoverable on our end.

↩  You receive a 80% refund of your total trek cost.
Less than 7 Days Before Departure
30% service charge

With less than a week to go, nearly all arrangements are locked in — your guide is ready, the route is planned, and teahouses are expecting you. A 50% charge applies to cover these committed costs.

↩  You receive a 70% refund of your total trek cost.
24 Hours or Less Before Departure
40% service charge

Cancellations within 24 hours carry the same 50% charge. At this stage your guide has travelled, logistics are in motion, and it is not possible to reassign resources elsewhere. We appreciate your understanding.

↩  You receive a 60% refund of your total trek cost.

Turning Back Early

Returning Midway

🏔️
No Refund on Trek Cost

If You Leave the Trail Early

If you begin a trek and need to return partway through — for any reason — the full trek cost is non-refundable. Our guides, teahouses, and logistics have already been deployed for your journey.

❌

No refund on remaining trek days

Once a trek has begun, the cost is fully committed regardless of how many days remain on the itinerary.

🚁

Emergency evacuation costs

Any costs related to emergency evacuation — helicopter, transport, or medical — are the responsibility of the trekker. We strongly recommend travel insurance that covers mountain evacuation.

🛡️

Travel insurance is strongly advised

We recommend all trekkers hold comprehensive travel insurance covering cancellation, medical emergency, and evacuation before departure.

🏨

Our Goodwill Offer — Pokhara Accommodation

Complimentary arrangement

While we cannot issue a refund for unused trek days, we will arrange comfortable hotel accommodation in Pokhara for the number of nights that remain on your itinerary — at no additional charge to you.

Please Note

A Few Important Details

📅

Date Changes

If you'd like to shift your trek dates rather than cancel outright, contact us as early as possible. We'll do our best to accommodate date changes without any penalty if notice is given well in advance.

🌧️

Weather & Force Majeure

In the unlikely event we cancel a trek due to severe weather, natural disaster, or safety concerns, you will receive a full refund or the option to reschedule at no extra cost.

💳

Refund Processing Time

Refunds are processed within 7–14 business days of cancellation confirmation. International transfers may take a few additional days depending on your bank.

✍️

How to Cancel

All cancellations must be submitted in writing via email to info@discoverparbat.com. The date of that email determines which charge tier applies.

Questions about your booking?

We're always happy to talk through your options — just write to us directly.
